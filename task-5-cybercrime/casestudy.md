# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI/Online Payment Fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, where attackers trick people into transferring money through fake payment requests, phishing links, or scam calls. This crime mainly targets students, working professionals, and elderly people who frequently use digital payment apps.

A typical UPI fraud happens step by step. First, the attacker sends a fake message, call, or link pretending to be a bank employee, delivery agent, or customer support representative. Second, the victim is asked to click a link or scan a QR code to receive money, verify KYC, or claim a refund. Third, when the victim enters UPI PIN or approves the payment request, money is transferred to the attacker's account. In some cases, scammers create fake websites or apps that look like real banking or payment platforms to steal login details.

Students and online shoppers are usually targeted because they frequently use UPI apps and may not fully understand digital payment security. The consequences include financial loss, account compromise, and mental stress. Sometimes victims lose their entire savings and face difficulty recovering money.

UPI fraud cases are increasing due to lack of awareness and careless online behavior. Therefore, understanding digital payment safety and verifying sources before making transactions is very important.
